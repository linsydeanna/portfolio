A little about me: I am transitioning into tech after a variety of experiences. I spent the last five years working at a private K-8th grade school where I was the coordinator of the after-school and summer programs. I absolutely loved working with children and making them laugh with my terrible attempts at accents. While I was working at the school, I completed a master's degree in counseling psychology and became fascinated by personality theory. It was then that I began to realize that my affinity for logic, analysis and creativity is a perfect fit with technology. I have always enjoyed thinking and coming up with disruptive ideas. In my early twenties, I performed aerial acrobatics at Sea World Orlando and in my hometown at local events. I still enjoy trying a scary drop on silks or seeing if I can get a difficult strength move on trapeze. A challenge is what makes me tick. For about a year, I explored entrepreneurship. This is actually what led me to discover the coding boot camp I graduated from. While I was taking a startup course, I noticed a place across the hall called The Iron Yard. I enrolled in July of 2016 and graduated in October after building a strong foundational knowledge of front end web development. I spent my twenties searching for a best-fit career and refusing to settle for less. I have found this in web development.
